Mac Apps

A list of third-party Mac software you may not know about but should.

  • User Experience
  • Utility

SuperDuper
The hard drive(s) or SSD in your computer will fail on you one day. If the drive just contains media files, it's easy to back it up simply by copying and pasting the files to another drive. However, if you need to be able to immediately start working again, then you need a backup boot drive that has all of your system info and user configurations intact that you can swap with your broken drive. Time Machine can back up your files but you still need to reinstall Mac OS X on a drive before you can restore the files. SuperDuper can create a backup boot drive that lets you start working again right away. There's also a free alternative to SuperDuper that I've heard good thing about from other users called Carbon Copy Cloner; it is supposed to do the same thing, but I haven't used it and therefore can't comment on its reliability like I can with SuperDuper which has been solid for me.
OmniDiskSweeper
Do you often run low on storage space on your hard drive and wonder what's taking up all that space? OmniDiskSweeper makes figuring that out quick and painless.
iStat Menus
Displays your system's activity in your status bar. If your computer is running slowly or hot, you can diagnose it quickly by looking at what's consuming the most resources in the CPU or RAM menu.
