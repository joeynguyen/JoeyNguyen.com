SuperDuper
The hard drive(s) or SSD in your computer will fail on you one day; it's an inevitability you should prepare for. If the drive just contains media or document files, it's easy to back it up simply by copying and pasting the files to another drive or using Time Machine. However, if you need to be able to immediately start working again, then you need a backup boot drive that has all of your system info and user configurations intact that you can swap with your broken drive. Time Machine can back up your files, but it can't make a backup boot drive that lets you swap into your computer and start working right away; you still need to reinstall Mac OS X on a drive first before you can restore your files from Time Machine.
SuperDuper's exists to create a backup boot drive that you can swap with your broken drive and begin working again immediately. There's also a free alternative to SuperDuper that I've heard good thing about from other Mac users called Carbon Copy Cloner. It's supposed to do the same thing SuperDuper does, but I haven't used it consistently enough to comment on its reliability like I can with SuperDuper which has been rock-solid for me.




OmniDiskSweeper
Do you often run low on storage space on your hard drive and wonder what's taking up all that space? OmniDiskSweeper makes figuring that out ridiculously easy.




iStat Menus
iStat Menus displays your system's activity in your status bar. If your computer is running slowly or hot, you can diagnose it quickly by looking at what's consuming the most resources in the CPU or RAM menu. It can also display your computer's temperature, network activity, and battery health.
