Mac Apps

A list of third-party Mac software you may not know about but should.

  • User Experience
  • Utility
  • System
  • Security
Name Mangler
Ever need to rename a large batch of files quickly? This free tool will let you do that. Number files sequentially, insert/remove/replace multiple characters at once, and several other methods of renaming large amounts of files.
Dropbox
If you regularly use more than one computer (laptop and desktop or personal and work), Dropbox may be your new best friend. It creates a folder within your user home folder, and any file you put in that folder will seamlessly sync between all computers you have Dropbox installed on.
Click here and here for some suggestions on how to use it to make life more convenient for you. Then click here to use my referral link. Dropbox comes with 2GB of free storage, but when someone signs up through a referral link, both that person and the referrer receive an additional 250MB of free storage!
Pixelmator
Professional graphic designers and photographers may need all of Adobe Photoshop's amazing features for their work and therefore can justify the cost. However, for those of us who don't need to rely on it to make a living but still want a great graphic editing software without shelling out too much dough, there aren't many choices on a Mac.
I've tried GIMP, Seashore, and Pinta but none of them had the feel of a good graphic editor. Pixelmator, on the other hand is THE best Mac alternative to Photoshop in terms of look, ease of use, and features and can be had for a very affordable price.
TextWrangler
I may spend more of my time coding for websites in Coda, but if I had to give up one of the two, I would rather part ways with Coda than TextWrangler. For a free tool, TextWrangler has so many powerful features, many of which I haven't even tried yet.
Not only is it great at editing code, TextWrangler comes in handy for many other job functions as well. Some of its features which I have found indispensable include Multi-File Search, Find Differences (between files), Soft Wrap Text, and Process Duplicate Lines.
VirtualBox
Compared to its paid alternatives, VMware Fusion and Parallels, VirtualBox is lacking in some of the more fancy features, but if you just need to be able to access a Windows or Linux (or even another Mac OS X) desktop to run a program only supported by that OS, VirtualBox is free and extremely capable.
Flux
Does one thing and does it well: at night when your eyes are tired, Flux adds a warm tint to your monitor's display to reduce the headache you may get from staring at a bright white-lit screen.
