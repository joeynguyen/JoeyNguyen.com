Alfred
Alfred is like a Swiss-army knife for OS X and a keyboard-shortcut enthusiast's dream application. Its free version is fairly useful, but its paid "Powerpack" version is where it really shines. Command-line-style File System Navigation, built-in Clipboard History, and the ability to search one of many popular sites like Amazon, eBay, and YouTube with just a keyboard shortcut are some of the many features that make Alfred great.




Pixelmator
Professional graphic designers and photographers may need all of Adobe Photoshop's super crazy features for their work and therefore can justify the high cost. However, for those of us who don't need to rely on it to make a living but still want a great graphic editing software without shelling out too much dough, there aren't many good choices on a Mac.
I've tried GIMP, Seashore, Acorn, and Pinta but none of them had the combination of usable interface, advanced features, and good looks (I'm shallow) of a great graphic editor. To me, Pixelmator is THE best inexpensive alternative to Photoshop I've found on the Mac OS.




Name Mangler
Ever need to rename a large batch of files? Name Mangler can help you do that quickly and efficiently. Number Files Sequentially, Find and Replace, and Insert/Remove Characters are among several methods of renaming multiple files in one quick sweep.




TextWrangler
I spend more of my time coding for websites in Coda, but if I had to give up one of my two most used code editors, I would rather part ways with Coda than TextWrangler. For a free tool, TextWrangler has so many powerful features, many of which I haven't even tried yet.
Not only is it good for writing code, TextWrangler comes in handy for many other job functions as well. Some of its features which I have found indispensable include Multi-File Search, Find Differences (between text files), Soft Wrap Text, and Process Duplicate Lines.




VirtualBox
Compared to its paid alternatives, VMware Fusion and Parallels, VirtualBox lacks some of the more fancy features. However, if you just need to be able to access a Windows or Linux (or even another Mac OS X) desktop to run a program only supported by that OS or for doing testing, VirtualBox is free and extremely capable.




f.lux
F.lux does one thing and does it well: at night, when your eyes are tired, you can set it to add a warm tint to your computer screen's display to reduce the headache you may get from staring at a bright back-lit screen. You can also adjust the level of the tint with the program.
Studies indicate that watching TV or using a computer/smartphone at night right before going to bed makes it harder to fall asleep. It's hypothesized that the bright light from the screen is partly to blame so f.lux was created to help with this. Dropbox
If you regularly use more than one computer (laptop and desktop or personal and work), Dropbox may be your new best friend. It creates a folder within your user home folder, and any file you put in that folder will seamlessly sync between all computers you have Dropbox installed on. You can choose to share certain folders in your Dropbox directory with other Dropbox users like picture albums or a project you're collaborating on.
